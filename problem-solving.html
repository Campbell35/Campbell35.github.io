My approach to proplem solving

  • The time I got blocked on a simple Problem

    • A couple of months before foundations had started, I was emersed in self directed study, trying my best to learn as much as I could to try and build an understanding of how a webpage is created. So I thought it maybe a good idea to build one myself, long story short, the webpage eventually started to come to something but wasn't very exciting, after some research I found a mini project which was some bouncing balls which I thought looked pretty cool and thought that they would add something fun to my webpage, so I followed a tutorial on how to do it only to come to the end of the tutorial to find that it did not work but it did for the guy demonstrating it. I had two options one would be to got back throught the tutorial and restart all over again, the second would be to try figure this out for myself which is what I decided to do. The first step in solving this issue was for me to get familiar with error messages and build an understanding of what they meant so after a bit of research I now had a bit of an idea of what had happened, as it turned out it was something that I had missed which was a little bit of a relief. I went through everything jotting down off the top of my head what it could be that is missing, then something clicked, the HTML document needed to be parsed fully in order for the the javascript code to implement the animation which made total sense even though I really had no idea why, in the script tag I put defer src instead of just src and it worked. I got pretty fustrated throughout that senario but had determination to get it sorted and new I would feel a sense of achievment after it had been solved. I then realised and learned that even when you follow a tutorial by an experienced developer they also can make mistakes or in this case leave something out so always be prepared to face solving a problem when you least expecdt it.
